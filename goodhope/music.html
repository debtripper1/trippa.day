music
00. Saying Goodbye — single, 1996
The first recording. Standalone. Recorded before the project had a name. Possibly a goodbye to someone, possibly to something else.

01. All My Friends Are Dead and Dying — LP, 1997
Eight songs about friendship and the people we outlive. The title is literal. Highlights include "Fox and the Hound" (a eulogy for a friendship), "Grieving In Advance" (anticipatory grief and guilt), and "Warm in the Dark" (struggling to find a reason to live).

02. A Little Spiral of My Own — LP, 1997
Seven songs about addiction, performance, and the slow erasure of self. Written in bars, hotel rooms, and the back of a car between New Orleans and San Francisco. Contains "Circling The Drain" and "Ghost" — two of the project's heaviest songs.

03. Everything Will Be Okay, In The End — LP, 1997
Eight songs about the possibility of an ending. Closes with "Bloom" — the first song in the catalogue that sounds like it might want to continue living. The title is not a guarantee. It is a question the artist is still trying to answer.

"I'm trying hard to believe, I'm finding a reason to live that isn't for my family"
— Warm in the Dark
