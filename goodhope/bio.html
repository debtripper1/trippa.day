bio

The artist behind Good Hope remains unnamed. The project began in 1996 with a single called "Saying Goodbye" and expanded to three full-length records in 1997. All three were recorded in the same year. The artist has not explained why.

There is no live band. There has never been a live performance. There are no plans for either. The music exists only in recordings made alone, at night, in spaces that were not designed for recording.

The lyrics deal with loss, addiction, suicidal ideation, and the slow erosion of self. The titles of the albums — All My Friends Are Dead and Dying, A Little Spiral of My Own, Everything Will Be Okay, In The End — form a narrative arc that the artist has not explicitly confirmed but has not denied either.

There is no label. No management. No publicist. No press kit. The music was released independently on digital platforms and this website.
