/// WHO
Good Hope is the musical project of one person. Or maybe more. The name has appeared on three full-length records, a handful of singles, and a collection of fragments that may or may not ever see release.

The music exists somewhere between folk and noise, between acoustic confession and digital decay. Songs are built from loops, field recordings, late-night voice memos, and whatever instrument was within arm's reach.
/// WHY
The lyrics speak for themselves — loss, addiction, the slow erosion of self, the terrifying prospect of being known. There is no persona here, no character. The songs are documents of a person trying to stay alive, and often failing at it.

The title of the third album — Everything Will Be Okay, In The End — is not a promise. It is something someone said once, and the artist is repeating it back to see if it sounds true.
/// WHERE
Recorded in bedrooms, basements, garages, and one hotel room in New Orleans. Mixed alone, at night. Mastered by nobody in particular.

The project takes its name not from optimism, but from the gap between what we hope for and what we get.

There is no label. No management. No press kit.
